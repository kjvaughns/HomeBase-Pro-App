# HomeBase Provider Portal MVP

## Core principle

The provider portal exists to:

1. Get providers paid
2. Keep their schedule organized
3. Make HomeBase feel like a real business tool, not a toy

If it does not help a provider **make money or save time**, it is not MVP.

---

## 1. Authentication and Account Basics REQUIRED

Must work flawlessly.

* Provider signup
* Login
* Password reset
* Ability to exist as both homeowner and provider under one account
* Profile switching must work instantly

Failure here kills trust immediately.

---

## 2. Provider Onboarding MVP

Purpose: Get a provider ready to accept jobs fast.

Required steps:

1. Welcome screen explaining how HomeBase helps them get jobs and get paid
2. Business basics

   * Business name
   * Service category (single or multiple)
   * Service area (city or radius)
3. Payout setup prompt (can be “connect later”)
4. Done → Provider dashboard

Do NOT:

* Force logo upload
* Force long surveys
* Force service pricing logic yet

Goal: First job possible within minutes.

---

## 3. Provider Dashboard (Home Tab)

This is their command center.

Must show:

* Revenue MTD
* Jobs completed
* Active clients
* Upcoming appointments

Optional but good:

* Attention cards (unpaid invoice, confirm appointment)

Every card must link somewhere real.

---

## 4. Schedule (Schedule Tab)

This is the heart of the app.

Must support:

* Day and list view
* View upcoming jobs
* Job detail page
* Job status changes

  * Scheduled
  * In progress
  * Completed

Required actions:

* Add job
* Reschedule job
* Mark complete

If schedule is broken or confusing, providers churn.

---

## 5. Client Management (Clients Tab)

Absolute MVP requirement.

Must support:

* Client list
* Add client
* Client detail page

  * Contact info
  * Job history
  * Invoice history

Clients must be reusable across jobs.

This is where your CRM credibility lives.

---

## 6. Job Creation Flow MUST WORK

Non negotiable.

Required:

1. Select existing client or create new
2. Select service
3. Select date and time
4. Add notes
5. Create job

Once created:

* Job appears in schedule
* Client is linked
* Can generate invoice later

No AI required yet.

---

## 7. Invoices and Payments (Money Tab)

This is critical for adoption.

Must support:

* Create invoice
* Send invoice
* Mark paid
* View payment history

Stripe integration can be basic:

* Payment link
* Manual mark paid

If providers cannot get paid easily, they leave.

---

## 8. Services Management (Simple MVP)

Keep it light.

Must support:

* Create service

  * Name
  * Description
  * Base price or “quote required”
* Edit service
* Select service during job creation

No dynamic pricing yet.

---

## 9. Marketplace Profile (Read Only MVP)

Purpose: Let providers look legit.

Must show:

* Business name
* Description
* Services offered
* Service area

Publishing toggle can exist but does not need ranking logic.

---

## 10. Notifications MVP

Bare minimum:

* New booking
* Job reminder
* Payment received

Push or in app is fine. One must work.

---

## 11. Settings and More Tab

Keep it clean.

Must include:

* Business profile
* Payout settings
* Switch to homeowner
* Support
* Logout

Avoid overloading this tab.

---

## 12. Data Model MVP (Critical)

This determines if HomeBase scales or collapses.

Required entities:

* Provider
* Client
* Job
* Service
* Invoice
* Payment

Relationships must be:
Provider → Clients → Jobs → Invoices → Payments

No shortcuts here.

---

## What is NOT MVP for Provider

Be brutal.

Not MVP:

* Teams
* AI job assignment
* Route optimization
* Advanced analytics
* Subscription plans
* Automation rules
* CRM insights

These come after providers trust the platform.

---

## MVP Success Criteria

You are provider-ready when:

* A provider can sign up
* Add a client
* Create a job
* Complete the job
* Send an invoice
* Get paid

If any of those steps fail or feel janky, **do not onboard more providers**.
