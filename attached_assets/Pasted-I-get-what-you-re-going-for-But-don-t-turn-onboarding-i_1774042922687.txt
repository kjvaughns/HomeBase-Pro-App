I get what you’re going for. But don’t turn onboarding into a movie.

Providers don’t care about “immersive.”
They care about: **more jobs, less hassle, getting paid faster.**

So we’ll build something that *feels premium and motivating*, but still gets them set up fast.

Here’s the right prompt:

---

## 🔧 Prompt: AI-Powered Provider Onboarding (High-Converting, Not Fluffy)

Redesign the HomeBase provider onboarding into a fast, high-impact, AI-assisted setup experience that helps service providers go from signup → ready to get booked in minutes.

The onboarding should feel premium and motivating, but must stay efficient and outcome-driven.

---

## Core Goal

By the end of onboarding, the provider must:

* have a complete business profile
* have at least 1 service created
* have availability set
* have a live booking link
* understand how they will get jobs and get paid

If this is not achieved, onboarding failed.

---

## Key User Psychology (Home Service Providers)

Design messaging around what they actually care about:

* getting more jobs consistently
* not chasing leads
* saving time on admin work
* looking more professional
* getting paid faster and reliably
* not dealing with complicated tools

Avoid generic startup language.

Speak directly to:

* solo operators
* small teams
* guys running real service businesses (landscaping, cleaning, HVAC, etc.)

---

## Onboarding Structure (Step by Step)

### Step 0: Emotional Hook (Short, not cheesy)

Full screen, clean design:

Headline:
“Get booked. Stay organized. Get paid.”

Subtext:
“Everything you need to run your business, in one place.”

CTA: Continue

Optional subtle animation, keep it under 2 seconds.

---

### Step 1: Business Setup

Fields:

* business name
* service category
* service area

AI Enhancements:

* auto-suggest category
* auto-format name
* small tip:
  “Clear business names get more bookings”

---

### Step 2: Services Setup (Most Important Step)

Simple, fast UI:

* add service name
* price or “quote required”
* duration

AI Enhancements:

* suggest common services based on category
* suggest pricing ranges
* improve service names (clean, professional)
* auto-generate description if missing

NO chat UI.

Inline suggestions only.

---

### Step 3: Availability

* set working hours
* simple toggle UI

AI Enhancements:

* suggest “standard” hours
* tip:
  “Providers with consistent availability get booked more”

---

### Step 4: Business Profile Polish

Fields:

* description
* optional logo/photo

AI Enhancements:

* “Improve description” button
* rewrite to:

  * better grammar
  * clearer value
  * more professional tone

Keep provider voice, don’t make it sound robotic.

---

### Step 5: Live Preview (Important)

Show:

“This is what customers will see”

Display:

* services
* pricing
* availability

This builds trust and excitement.

---

### Step 6: Payments Setup

Simple explanation:

“Get paid directly through HomeBase”

* connect Stripe (or mock)
* minimal friction

---

### Step 7: Value Reinforcement (Short + Visual)

Small visual section:

* “Save hours every week”
* “Stop chasing leads”
* “Get booked automatically”

Use subtle animations or icons.

DO NOT drag this out.

---

### Step 8: Paywall + Free Trial

Clear value:

“You’re ready to start getting booked”

* Free trial CTA (primary)
* Plan details (secondary)

No confusion.

---

### Step 9: Activation Screen (Most Important Moment)

Headline:
“You’re live”

Show:

* booking link
* copy/share button

CTA:
“Send your link and get your first booking”

---

## AI Behavior Rules

AI must:

* be instant
* be helpful
* not require effort

Use:

* autofill
* suggestions
* rewrite buttons
* small tips

DO NOT:

* use chat-based flows
* force user to interact with AI
* slow down onboarding

---

## Design Requirements

* one step per screen
* progress bar at top
* smooth transitions
* premium, minimal UI
* no clutter
* no long paragraphs

Animations:

* subtle only
* fast
* purposeful

---

## What to Avoid

* long “motivational” speeches
* fake hype language
* too many steps
* complex forms
* anything that delays setup

---

## Deliverables

1. full onboarding flow rebuilt
2. AI enhancements integrated inline
3. improved copy focused on real provider needs
4. removed unnecessary steps
5. working end-to-end onboarding

---

## ⚠️ Real talk

You’re very close to overdoing it again.

If onboarding:

* feels long
* feels like a tutorial
* feels like a pitch

people will drop.

The “this is going to change your business” feeling should come from:
→ how easy setup is
→ how clean it looks
→ how fast they’re ready to get booked

Not from big promises.

---

## One decision you need to make

Do you want onboarding to feel more:

A. Fast and practical
B. Slightly emotional and inspiring

Pick one.

You can’t fully optimize both without hurting conversion.
