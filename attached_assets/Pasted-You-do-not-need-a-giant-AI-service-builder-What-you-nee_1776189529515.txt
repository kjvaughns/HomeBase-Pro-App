You do **not** need a giant AI service builder.

What you need is one feature:

## **Smart Service Blueprint**

It is basically an AI assisted service setup flow that helps a provider create a service with:

1. **how they price**
2. **what info they need before booking**
3. **what upsells or add ons they offer**
4. **when the job should switch from instant price to custom quote**

That solves the real problem.

Because the issue is not just “create a service.”
The issue is:

**providers need a simple way to turn their real world pricing logic into a clean booking flow without manually building every rule.**

## What it should do

When a provider creates a service, they choose something like:

* House Cleaning
* Lawn Mowing
* Pest Control
* Pressure Washing
* Handyman
* Junk Removal

Then AI generates a **Service Blueprint** with 4 sections:

### 1. Pricing Model

The app asks:

**How do you usually charge for this service?**

Options:

* Flat rate
* Starts at price
* Per square foot
* By property size
* By room count
* By hourly rate
* Custom quote only
* Mixed pricing

Then AI recommends the most common setup based on category.

Example for cleaning:

* base price by home size
* modifiers for bedrooms and bathrooms
* clean type selector
* add ons like oven, fridge, deep clean

Example for lawn care:

* flat rate or lot size based
* front yard only or front and back
* frequency discount
* add ons like edging, hedge trim, leaf cleanup

## 2. Required Intake Questions

This is where your booking AI and service creator connect.

The provider should be able to choose:

**What do you need to know before accepting this job?**

AI prebuilds smart questions based on category.

Cleaning example:

* How many bedrooms?
* How many bathrooms?
* Approximate square footage?
* What type of cleaning?
* Any pets?
* Do you want inside oven or fridge cleaned?

Lawn example:

* Approximate lot size?
* Front yard only or full yard?
* Is the grass overgrown?
* Any gates or access issues?
* Do you want edging?
* Do you want recurring service?

Pest control example:

* What pest issue are you dealing with?
* Inside, outside, or both?
* How severe is the issue?
* One time or recurring?
* Is this residential or commercial?

This is huge because it makes the intake actually useful instead of generic.

## 3. Add Ons and Upsells

This is where you help providers make more money.

After AI builds the main service, it should suggest:

* common add ons
* recurring options
* premium upgrades

Examples:

Cleaning:

* deep clean
* inside fridge
* inside oven
* move in or move out clean
* same day rush

Lawn:

* edging
* bush trimming
* weed treatment
* leaf removal
* monthly recurring plan

Pest:

* mosquito treatment
* monthly prevention
* rodent exclusion
* attic inspection

This matters because most providers leave money on the table by only listing the basic service.

## 4. Booking Logic

This is the most important part.

The provider decides whether the service should be:

* **Instant Book with exact price**
* **Starts at price and final confirmation later**
* **Request quote only**

That keeps it simple while still flexible.

Because not every service should auto quote.

Example:

* basic lawn mow = instant price
* deep cleaning = starts at price
* heavy pest infestation = request quote

That prevents bad quotes and protects provider profit.

Preview booking flow

That preview part is non negotiable. They need confidence.

# Why this is better than a normal service creator

Because most service creators make providers do too much manual setup.

Yours should do the thinking first, then let them approve it.

That means the provider is not building from scratch.
They are **editing an intelligent draft**.

That is the sweet spot.

## The core principle

**AI suggests. Provider approves.**

Not:

* AI fully controls everything
* provider manually builds every field

That covers a ton of service businesses without overcomplicating the app.
