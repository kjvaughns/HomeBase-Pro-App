## 🔧 Provider Audit + Onboarding Build Prompt

Analyze the entire HomeBase provider side codebase and redesign it to support a clean, high-converting onboarding flow that leads providers to their first real outcome: getting booked and getting paid.

---

## Phase 1: Full Provider Side Audit

Scan the entire provider portal and identify:

### 1. UX Problems

* confusing navigation
* cluttered screens
* unclear actions
* too many features too early
* anything that would overwhelm a new provider

### 2. Product Gaps

* missing steps required to:

  * accept bookings
  * manage schedule
  * get paid
* unclear setup requirements
* anything that would block a provider from going live

### 3. Overbuilt / Useless Features

* features that are not needed for MVP
* duplicate functionality
* AI features that don’t provide real value
* anything that slows down onboarding

### 4. Technical Issues

* broken flows
* bad state management
* unnecessary complexity
* slow loading areas
* poor routing between screens

---

## Output Required (Audit)

Return:

1. Top 10 issues blocking provider activation
2. Features to REMOVE or postpone
3. Features that must be simplified
4. Critical missing pieces for MVP

---

## Phase 2: Define Ideal Provider Onboarding Flow

Design a step-by-step onboarding that gets a provider from signup → ready to accept bookings.

The onboarding must be short, clear, and outcome-driven.

---

## Core Goal

By the end of onboarding, the provider must have:

* at least 1 service created
* availability set
* booking link generated
* payment setup ready (real or mocked)

If this is not achieved, onboarding is incomplete.

---

## Required Onboarding Steps

### Step 1: Business Basics

* business name
* service category
* service area

Keep it fast. Autofill where possible.

---

### Step 2: Services Setup

* create at least 1 service
* required:

  * name
  * price or “quote required”
  * duration

Provide smart defaults to reduce friction.

---

### Step 3: Availability

* working hours
* basic schedule

Keep UI extremely simple.

---

### Step 4: Booking Preview

* show their live booking page
* let them see exactly what customers will see

This builds confidence.

---

### Step 5: Payments Setup

* connect Stripe (or mock for now)
* explain simply:
  “Get paid directly through HomeBase”

---

### Step 6: Paywall + Trial

* show clear value:

  * get booked
  * manage clients
  * get paid
* offer free trial as primary CTA
* secondary: view plans

---

### Step 7: Activation Screen

* “You’re live”
* show:

  * booking link
  * copy/share button
* CTA:
  “Send your link and get your first booking”

---

## Phase 3: UX + Design Requirements

* One step per screen
* Progress indicator at top
* No overwhelming forms
* Use defaults wherever possible
* Allow skipping non-critical fields
* Clean, modern, premium design

---

## Phase 4: AI Integration (Use Carefully)

Only include AI where it actually reduces effort:

* suggest service names and pricing
* auto-generate descriptions
* recommend availability blocks
* help write bio

Do NOT force AI into every step.

---

## Phase 5: Simplification Rules

* Remove anything not required to:
  get booked → manage job → get paid

* If a feature does not help a provider make money or save time immediately, move it out of onboarding

---

## Phase 6: Deliverables

1. Full audit report of provider side
2. Clean onboarding flow structure
3. Updated screen breakdown (step by step)
4. List of removed or postponed features
5. Rebuilt onboarding UX (connected and working)
6. Any remaining risks or blockers

---

## Non Negotiable

This onboarding must make a provider feel:

“I can actually use this to run my business today”

Not:
“This looks cool but I don’t know where to start”

---

## ⚠️ Reality check for you

If your onboarding takes more than 2 to 3 minutes to complete, you’re losing people.

If they don’t see a booking link at the end, you failed.

If they don’t understand how they make money using your app, you failed.
