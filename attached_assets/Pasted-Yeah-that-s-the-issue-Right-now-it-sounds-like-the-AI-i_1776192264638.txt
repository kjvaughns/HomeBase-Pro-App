Yeah, that’s the issue.

Right now it sounds like the AI is acting like the boss instead of a tool. Business owners will hate that fast.

They want to feel like:

**“I’m building my service. HomeBase is just making it easier.”**

Not:

**“This app is trying to decide how I run my business.”**

## What needs to change

### 1. AI should be secondary, not primary

The screen should not feel like an AI chat takeover.

Instead of:

* AI asking everything
* AI pushing recommendations everywhere
* AI generating the whole service in a dominant way

Make it:

* provider chooses the service structure
* AI offers optional suggestions inside each section
* provider stays in control the whole time

So the framing changes from:

**“Let AI create your service”**

to:

**“Build your service with optional AI help”**

That is a huge difference.

## 2. The UI should feel like a normal builder first

The base experience should feel like a clean service setup form.

For example:

### Page structure

* Service Name
* Category
* Pricing Type
* What should customers select?
* Add ons
* Booking settings

Then inside each section, have a small AI assist button like:

* Suggest options
* Recommend questions
* Help me price this
* Generate common add ons

That feels way better than making AI the center of the whole experience.

## 3. Remove the “AI is talking at me” feeling

Do not make every page sound like a chatbot.

Business owners do not want to feel like they are being interviewed by a robot just to make a lawn mowing service.

The tone should be more like:

* “Choose how you price this service”
* “Add the details customers need to provide”
* “Offer add ons to increase ticket size”

Then a smaller assist option:

* “Get AI suggestions”

That is enough.

## 4. The provider should always make the first decision

AI should react to their choices, not lead everything from the front.

Example:

Provider picks:

* Cleaning
* Pricing by home size
* Add on options enabled

Then AI says:

* “Based on cleaning businesses, you may want to ask bedrooms, bathrooms, and clean type.”
* “Want to add these?”

That feels like assistance.

Not control.

## 5. Stop hiding progress and actions

The green bar with no words is weak UX.

Every page needs a clear action button at the bottom.

Use simple CTA language like:

* Continue
* Next step
* Save and continue
* Review service
* Publish service

No silent green bars.
That makes the app feel unfinished and confusing.

## 6. Give them visible ownership language

Everywhere in the flow, the wording should reinforce that this is **their** service.

Use lines like:

* Your service setup
* Your pricing rules
* What customers need to answer
* Choose how you want to charge
* Add optional upgrades

Avoid wording like:

* AI generated for you
* Recommended flow selected
* Smart setup decided
* Optimized automatically

That wording feels controlling.

## Best structure instead

## Service Creator should be 3 layers

### Layer 1: Core manual setup

Provider manually picks:

* service name
* category
* pricing style
* booking type

### Layer 2: AI assistance

Optional help buttons inside each section:

* Suggest pricing structure
* Suggest intake questions
* Suggest add ons
* Suggest upsells

### Layer 3: Final review

Show them a clean summary:

* Base service
* Pricing method
* Customer questions
* Add ons
* Booking flow

Then let them edit anything.

That makes AI feel like an assistant, not a manager.

## The rule you need to follow

**AI should recommend, never assume.**
**AI should simplify, never override.**
**AI should stay one step behind the provider, not one step ahead.**

That’s the product truth here.

## What not to do

Do not do:

* full AI chat onboarding for service creation
* long AI generated paragraphs
* AI auto building the whole service before the provider decides anything
* hidden CTA buttons
* overexplaining why AI picked something

That all creates friction.

## The feeling you want

Not:
**“AI is building my service.”**

But:
**“I’m quickly setting up my service, and HomeBase helps when I need it.”**

That is the right product feel for business owners.

## blunt truth

If it feels too AI, it will feel unserious.
And if it feels unserious, providers will not trust it with pricing.

That is the danger.

For something tied to money, pricing, and bookings, the product has to feel:

* clear
* direct
* editable
* owner controlled

AI should just make it faster.
